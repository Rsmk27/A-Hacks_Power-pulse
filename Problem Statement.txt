Problem Statement

"When firefighters enter a burning building, the incident commander standing outside becomes completely blind — no location, no vitals, no awareness of whether the firefighter is safe or in danger. This information blackout costs lives."

Problem Explanation

Firefighting is one of the most dangerous jobs in the world. Every time a firefighter steps inside a burning building, they walk into an environment that is actively trying to kill them — extreme heat, toxic smoke, zero visibility, unstable structures, and rapidly changing conditions.
The officer commanding the operation stands outside. They coordinate the team, manage resources, and make critical decisions. But the moment a firefighter crosses that threshold into the building, the commander loses all real visibility into what is happening.
